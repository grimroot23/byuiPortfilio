Design created using fundamental HTML/CSS structures and implementing Bootstrap Grid/Flexbox to create pages that fit to any screen. All programming languages are written neatly and have notes for each part/tool involved in a design.

Essentially, responsive design is a way to put together a website so that it automatically scales its content and elements to match the screen size on which it is viewed. It keeps images from being larger than the screen width, and prevents visitors on mobile devices from needing to do extra work to read your content. The ultimate goal of responsive design is to avoid the unnecessary resizing, scrolling, zooming, or panning that occurs with sites that have not been optimized for different devices.

Often we find when we go to these smaller business websites, that they have an outdated website with no responsive elements, images (sizes may be too big, too small, or no longer working links), broken links, and outdated designs (Typography, Background, Font Colors, Layout).

My goal is to navigate and to take old HTML/CSS/Javascript from these websites, break it down and try to add new techniques/tools to improve its responsive nature across all screen sizes.
